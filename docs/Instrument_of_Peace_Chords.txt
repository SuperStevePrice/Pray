INSTRUMENT OF THY PEACE
St. Francis Prayer for Peace

TEXT: Prayer attributed to St. Francis of Assisi (12th-13th century, 
authorship disputed, but spiritually important) — public domain
ARRANGEMENT: Contemplative, simple arrangement
KEY: G major
TEMPO: ~62 BPM (peaceful, reflective, gentle)
TIME: 4/4

═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

LYRICS:

Lord, make me an instrument of Thy peace.
Where there is hatred, let me sow love.
Where there is injury, pardon.
Where there is doubt, faith.
Where there is despair, hope.
Where there is darkness, light.
Where there is sadness, joy.

O Divine Master, grant that I may not so much seek
To be consoled, as to console;
To be understood, as to understand;
To be loved, as to love.

For it is in giving that we receive,
It is in pardoning that we are pardoned,
And it is in dying that we are born to eternal life.

HISTORICAL/SPIRITUAL CONTEXT:

This prayer is attributed to St. Francis of Assisi (1181-1226), though modern
scholars debate authorship. It doesn't matter—what matters is that it captures
Franciscan spirituality perfectly:

- Love of peace (Francis made peace between warring families)
- Love of the poor and dispossessed
- Transformation through sacrifice
- Joy in simplicity
- Trust in divine providence

The prayer's structure is beautiful:
1. REQUEST (make me an instrument)
2. LITANY (where there is X, let me bring Y)
3. PARADOX (in giving we receive; in pardoning we are pardoned)
4. RESOLUTION (in dying we are born to eternal life)

═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

THEOLOGICAL DEPTH:

This prayer is about:
- PEACE: not the absence of conflict, but presence of justice and love
- TRANSFORMATION: being changed into an instrument of peace
- PARADOX: losing ourselves, we find ourselves; dying, we live eternally
- SERVICE: consoling rather than being consoled; understanding rather than understood

Each opposite pair (hatred/love, injury/pardon, doubt/faith, despair/hope,
darkness/light, sadness/joy) is a spiritual principle.

═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

PERFORMANCE APPROACH:

This prayer is most powerful when sung with:
- Gentle conviction (not wimpy, not aggressive)
- Clear intention (this is a request to be made into a peacemaker)
- Peaceful tone (the message and manner should align)
- Hope (the paradoxes are actually liberating, not burdensome)

Think: "I offer myself as a channel for God's peace, trusting that in
serving others, I will be transformed."
