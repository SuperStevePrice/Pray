RUTTER PIE JESU (from Requiem)
Chord Chart and Recording Guide for GarageBand

COMPOSER: John Rutter (1975)
KEY: G major (you can transpose)
TEMPO: ~60 BPM (gentle, flowing)
TIME: 3/4 (waltz)

═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

LYRICS (Latin):

Pie Jesu, Pie Jesu,
Pie Jesu, Domine.
Dona eis requiem,
Dona eis requiem.
Dona nobis pacem.

[Translation: Gentle Jesus, Gentle Jesus, Gentle Jesus, Lord. Grant them rest.
Grant them rest. Grant us peace.]

Verse 2 (12 measures): "Dona eis requiem, dona eis requiem"

GARAGEBAND RECORDING TIPS:

1. INSTRUMENTS:
   - Acoustic guitar (nylon strings preferred—warm, resonant)
   - Solo voice (soprano or tenor range works beautifully)
   - OPTIONAL: Add a pad (Strings → Warm Pad) at 15% volume for shimmer

2. VOCAL APPROACH:
   - This piece is VERY lyrical—think "cantabile" (singing style)
   - Don't rush—let each syllable breathe
   - "Pie Jesu" should feel almost like a question or plea
   - "Dona nobis pacem" (grant us peace) is the emotional resolution
   - Sing from the diaphragm, not the throat (this is a long, sustained line)

3. RECORDING SEQUENCE:
   - Record guitar twice: one for reference, one for final mix
   - Vocal: 2-3 takes, comp the best moments
   - Let the guitar and voice "talk" to each other—not rigid timing

4. MICROPHONE PLACEMENT:
   - Vocal: 8-10 inches from mic, slightly off-axis to reduce plosives
   - Guitar: 2-3 feet away, capturing the natural resonance

5. MIXING:
   - Vocal: slightly prominent (2-3 dB above guitar)
   - Vocal reverb: Cathedral or Room, 18-22% wet (this piece needs space)
   - Guitar: minimal reverb (8-10%) or dry
   - Master: gentle, warm tone—no compression

6. EXPORT:
   - AAC 256 kbps or .wav
   - File name: "Rutter_Pie_Jesu_Steve_Price_[DATE]"

INTERPRETATION NOTES:

Rutter's Pie Jesu is one of the most beautiful contemporary choral works.
It's often sung by soloists (soprano or countertenor traditionally).

KEY to performance:
- This is a meditation on mercy and peace
- Texture: smooth, legato, no vibrato (or minimal vibrato)
- Phrasing: long lines—think of it as one continuous prayer
- Emotional arc: start gentle → build slightly on "dona" → resolve peacefully

The piece works equally well in major keys (G, D, Bb) or minor (Em, Am).
You can transpose to whatever key sits in your vocal sweet spot.

TRANSPOSITION REFERENCE:

If G major is too high/low, try:
- D major: capo 2 (same shape, D-Bm-Em-A progression)
- Bb major: capo -2 (Bb-Gm-Cm-F progression)
- Em: relative minor (darker, introspective quality)
