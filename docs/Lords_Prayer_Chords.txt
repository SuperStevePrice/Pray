LYRICS (English):

Our Father, who art in heaven,
Hallowed be thy Name.
Thy kingdom come, thy will be done,
On earth as it is in heaven.
Give us this day our daily bread.
And forgive us our trespasses,
As we forgive those who trespass against us.
And lead us not into temptation,
But deliver us from evil.
For thine is the kingdom, and the power, and the glory,
Forever and ever. Amen.

GARAGEBAND RECORDING TIPS:

1. RECORDING APPROACH:
   - This is THE most important prayer in Christian tradition
   - Don't rush—each phrase has weight
   - This is steady, not dramatic (unlike some recordings that oversell emotion)
   - Tempo should feel like liturgical recitation + song

2. VOCAL STYLE:
   - Sing clearly enough that someone unfamiliar with the prayer can follow it
   - Don't mumble Latin-style; articulate English clearly
   - The Lord's Prayer is usually chanted (like liturgical speech)
   - Think: hymn tune + prayer = your approach

3. GUITAR:
   - Simple fingerpicking (not too complex)
   - Let it sit under the vocal—support, don't compete
   - Use warm nylon strings for resonance
   - Consistent rhythm anchors the prayer

4. MICROPHONE SETUP:
   - Vocal: 8-10 inches, standard position
   - Guitar: 2-3 feet away (capture the natural resonance)
   - Record in a space with some natural reverb (bathroom tiles work!)

5. RECORDING SEQUENCE:
   - Guitar first (2 takes)
   - Vocal: 2-3 takes of the full prayer
   - Comp the best—this is long, you'll have natural variation
   - This is one continuous piece (don't break between verses in mix)

6. MIXING:
   - Vocal: forward (3-4 dB above guitar)
   - Vocal reverb: Room or Chapel, 15-18%
   - Guitar: dry (8-10% reverb max)
   - Master: warm, clear, no limiting
   - Preserve the liturgical quality—not overly produced

7. EXPORT:
   - AAC 256 kbps or .wav
   - File name: "Lords_Prayer_Steve_Price_[DATE]"

THEOLOGICAL/LITURGICAL NOTES:

The Lord's Prayer is:
- The ONLY prayer explicitly taught by Jesus in scripture
- Central to Christian liturgy (recited in nearly every service worldwide)
- A complete prayer structure: address, petition, intercession, doxology

Your recording should honor this significance. Avoid:
- Oversentimental interpretation
- Slow, dragging tempos (this can feel maudlin)
- Excessive reverb (clarity matters here)

Instead, go for:
- Steady, prayerful pacing
- Clear diction (people should understand every word)
- Warm, inviting tone
- Liturgical simplicity
