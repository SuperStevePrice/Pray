SANCTUS (Holy, Holy, Holy)
Chord Chart and Recording Guide for GarageBand

TEXT: Traditional Gregorian liturgical text (public domain)
ARRANGEMENT: Gregorian chant setting (simplified for guitar)
KEY: G major (modal, like Gregorian chant)
TEMPO: ~60 BPM (solemn, reverent, timeless)
TIME: 4/4 (though Gregorian is technically free rhythm)

LYRICS (Latin):

Sanctus, Sanctus, Sanctus Dominus Deus Sabaoth.
Pleni sunt caeli et terra gloria tua.
Hosanna in excelsis.
Benedictus qui venit in nomine Domini.
Hosanna in excelsis.

[Holy, Holy, Holy Lord God of Hosts. Heaven and earth are full of Thy glory.
Hosanna in the highest. Blessed is He that comes in the name of the Lord.
Hosanna in the highest.]

FINGERSTYLE APPROACH (modal/Gregorian style):

This is NOT metrically strict. Gregorian chant floats rhythmically.
Try this approach:
- Long, sustained notes (whole notes, half notes)
- Minimal movement between chords
- Let the voice lead; guitar follows loosely
- Think "organ drone" rather than "rhythmic guitar"

Optional: Play Em or G as a pedal point under verses, letting the vocal melody
float above. This is very Gregorian—voice leads, accompaniment sustains.

═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

STRUCTURE (Flexible, Gregorian-inspired):

Intro (4-6 measures): Establish G major, slow and reverential
Verse 1 (12 measures): "Sanctus, Sanctus, Sanctus..." (triple invocation)
Verse 2 (12 measures): "Pleni sunt caeli..." (declaration of fullness)
Verse 3 (12 measures): "Hosanna in excelsis" (praise)
Verse 4 (12 measures): "Benedictus qui venit..." (blessing)
Verse 5 (12 measures): "Hosanna in excelsis" (repeat praise)
Coda (4 measures): G major, fade

Total length: ~6-7 minutes (can be flexible, as in Gregorian tradition)

GARAGEBAND RECORDING TIPS:

1. VOCAL APPROACH (CRITICAL FOR THIS PIECE):
   - This is SUNG CHANT, not a melody
   - Approach it like plainsong: meditative, steady, no vibrato (or minimal)
   - The triple "Sanctus" is the most important moment—sing each with weight
   - "Hosanna" should feel celebratory (unlike Sanctus, which is solemn)
   - Let the words themselves suggest phrasing, not a predetermined melody

2. GUITAR STYLE:
   - SPARSE and SUPPORTIVE
   - Consider playing just long G and D notes under the voice
   - Or use a simple arpeggio that repeats
   - Think "Gregorian accompaniment" not "folk guitar"
   - Nylon strings only—no brightness

3. RECORDING APPROACH:
   - This is best recorded with the voice leading and guitar supporting
   - Record guitar FIRST with very sparse, sustained notes
   - Then record vocal over it (multiple takes)
   - The vocal should feel free and unrushed

4. MICROPHONE SETUP:
   - Vocal: 10-12 inches (slightly further than other pieces, for a floated quality)
   - Guitar: 3-4 feet (background, very quiet)
   - Record in a space with natural reverb (cathedral-like if possible)

5. MIXING:
   - Vocal: DOMINANT (5-6 dB above guitar)
   - Guitar: way back in the mix, almost texture
   - Vocal reverb: Large Cathedral or Hall, 25-35% (this needs SPACE)
   - Guitar: barely audible, almost subliminal
   - Master: NO compression, preserve the floating quality

6. EXPORT:
   - .wav (lossless) preferred—quality matters
   - AAC 256 kbps minimum
   - File name: "Sanctus_Gregorian_Steve_Price_[DATE]"

GREGORIAN CHANT HISTORY:

The Sanctus is part of the Mass Ordinary (fixed texts sung at every Mass).
The Gregorian version is one of the oldest continuous musical traditions—
literally sung the same way for over 1,500 years.

Key qualities of Gregorian:
- Monophonic (single melodic line, no harmony)
- Liturgical Latin (proper pronunciation matters)
- Free rhythm (breath-driven, not metronomic)
- Meditative quality

Your guitar accompaniment honors this by staying very simple and supportive.

═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

PERFORMANCE STYLE:

Think "monk in a monastery" more than "concert singer." This is prayer
set to sound, not entertainment.

- Sing from the diaphragm (long, sustainable notes)
- Breathe naturally (follow the Latin text's phrasing)
- No vibrato (or very minimal—this is chant, not opera)
- Let the repetition of "Sanctus" (holy) be meditative
- "Hosanna" (save us/help us) is slightly more active energy
- "Benedictus" returns to reverent contemplation

═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

LITURGICAL CONTEXT:

The Sanctus is sung during the Eucharistic Prayer at every Catholic and
Anglican Mass. It's the moment when the congregation joins the heavenly
chorus of angels. This is extremely sacred space in Christian tradition.

Your recording should reflect this gravity and beauty.
