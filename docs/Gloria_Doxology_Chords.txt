LYRICS (Latin — traditional liturgical text):

Gloria Patri et Filio et Spiritui Sancto,
Sicut erat in principio et nunc et semper
Et in saecula saeculorum. Amen.

[English: Glory be to the Father, and to the Son, and to the Holy Ghost;
As it was in the beginning, is now, and ever shall be, world without end. Amen.]

GARAGEBAND RECORDING TIPS:

1. VOCAL APPROACH:
   - This is JOYFUL music—brightness, not darkness
   - The Gloria traditionally follows penitential prayers (Kyrie, Miserere)
   - Sing with clarity, energy, and positive tone
   - Emphasize the three "Amens" at the end (affirmation of eternity)

2. GUITAR STYLE:
   - Brighter, more energetic fingerpicking than Ave Maria or Miserere
   - Let the open D strings ring—this chord is your anchor
   - Use nylon or steel strings (steel gives more brightness here)
   - Rhythmic precision is important—this is not free-floating

3. RECORDING APPROACH:
   - This is shorter than Lord's Prayer or Psalm 23—easier to nail in fewer takes
   - Record 2-3 guitar takes
   - Vocal: 2-3 takes (the "Amen" section is triple, so get those solid)
   - Comp for consistency and energy

4. MICROPHONE SETUP:
   - Vocal: 8 inches (standard position, capture the brightness)
   - Guitar: 2-3 feet (open strings should ring clearly)
   - Bright space preferred (not a dead room)

5. MIXING:
   - Vocal: forward (3-4 dB above guitar)
   - Vocal reverb: Room, 10-12% (lighter than Miserere, more than Ave Maria)
   - Guitar: dry or minimal reverb (8%)
   - Master: bright, clear, celebratory quality
   - NO compression—preserve dynamic range

6. EXPORT:
   - AAC 256 kbps or .wav
   - File name: "Gloria_Doxology_Steve_Price_[DATE]"

LITURGICAL CONTEXT:

The Gloria Patri (Glory Be) is:
- Sung after every psalm in traditional church services
- A way of blessing/sanctifying the psalm that preceded it
- One of the oldest liturgical texts (origins in early church, ~2nd century)
- Often called the "Lesser Doxology" (vs. Gloria in excelsis, the "Greater Doxology")

Your recording should convey:
- Gratitude and celebration
- Affirmation of God's eternal nature ("world without end")
- Light and clarity (not heaviness)

- D (home, affirmation)

The harmonic movement mirrors the text: opening statement → historical reference
→ eternal affirmation → final Amen.
