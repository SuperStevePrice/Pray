LYRICS (Latin — traditional liturgical form):

Magnificat anima mea Dominum,
Et exsultavit spiritus meus in Deo salutari meo.

[My soul doth magnify the Lord, and my spirit hath rejoiced in God my Saviour.]

(Full text continues through verses of praise and affirmation of God's power
and mercy, especially toward the humble and lowly.)

GARAGEBAND RECORDING TIPS:

1. VOCAL APPROACH:
   - This is Mary's prayer of PRAISE and JOY (not sorrow or penitence)
   - The Magnificat is about God's power reversing worldly order
   - Sing with warmth, strength, and confidence (not timidity)
   - This piece suits strong, confident female voices best
   (but any voice can approach it with appropriate energy)

2. GUITAR STYLE:
   - Lyrical, flowing fingerpicking
   - C major is open and resonant—let it ring
   - This is longer than most pieces, so consistency matters
   - Use warm nylon strings

3. RECORDING APPROACH:
   - This is one of the longer pieces (like Psalm 23)
   - Record 2-3 guitar takes
   - Vocal: 2-3 full takes
   - Comp the best moments—allow natural variation over this length

4. MICROPHONE SETUP:
   - Vocal: 8 inches (warm, forward position)
   - Guitar: 2-3 feet (capture the resonance of open strings)
   - Record in a space with some natural warmth (living room ideal)

5. MIXING:
   - Vocal: forward (3-4 dB above guitar)
   - Vocal reverb: Room, 15-18% (this piece needs some space)
   - Guitar: minimal reverb (8-10%)
   - Master: warm, open, celebratory quality
   - Preserve dynamic range—no aggressive compression

6. EXPORT:
   - AAC 256 kbps or .wav
   - File name: "Magnificat_Marys_Song_Steve_Price_[DATE]"

BIBLICAL/SPIRITUAL CONTEXT:

The Magnificat (Luke 1:46-55) is Mary's prayer in response to the Annunciation.
It's one of the most important Marian prayers and is:
- Sung at Vespers (evening prayer) in traditional services
- Central to Catholic and Orthodox Marian devotion
- A prayer about reversing injustice ("putting down the mighty, lifting up the lowly")
- Mary's own words about her role as the Mother of God

Your recording should convey:
- Joy and confidence (this is NOT a lament)
- Strength and dignity
- Faith in God's justice and mercy
- Theological importance (not sentimental)
