NICENE CREED
Chord Chart and Recording Guide for GarageBand

TEXT: Traditional Nicene Creed (325 AD, Council of Nicaea) — public domain
ARRANGEMENT: Simple liturgical setting
KEY: F major
TEMPO: ~60 BPM (steady, declarative, affirmative)
TIME: 4/4

═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

LYRICS (English — modern translation):

We believe in one God, the Father almighty, maker of heaven and earth,
of all that is, seen and unseen.

We believe in one Lord, Jesus Christ, the only Son of God,
eternally begotten of the Father, God from God, Light from Light,
true God from true God, begotten, not made, of one Being with the Father.

Through him all things were made. For us and for our salvation
he came down from heaven: by the power of the Holy Spirit
he became incarnate from the Virgin Mary, and was made man.

For our sake he was crucified under Pontius Pilate;
he suffered death and was buried. On the third day he rose again
in accordance with the Scriptures; he ascended into heaven
and is seated at the right hand of the Father.

He will come again in glory to judge both the living and the dead;
his kingdom will have no end.

We believe in the Holy Spirit, the Lord, the giver of life,
who proceeds from the Father and the Son. With the Father and the Son
he is worshipped and glorified. He has spoken through the Prophets.

We believe in one holy catholic and apostolic Church.
We acknowledge one baptism for the forgiveness of sins.
We look for the resurrection of the dead, and the life of the world to come.
Amen.

STRUCTURE:

Intro (8 measures): Establish F major, firm and confident
Verse 1 (16 measures): "We believe in one God..." (first article of faith)
Verse 2 (24 measures): "We believe in one Lord Jesus Christ..." (longest, most central)
Verse 3 (16 measures): "We believe in the Holy Spirit..." (third article)
Verse 4 (12 measures): "We believe in one holy catholic Church..." (conclusion)
Coda (4 measures): "Amen" — final affirmation

Total length: ~7-8 minutes (this is LONG—it's a complete creed)

GARAGEBAND RECORDING TIPS:

1. VOCAL APPROACH:
   - This is a STATEMENT OF FAITH—not tentative, not uncertain
   - Sing with clarity, strength, and conviction
   - This is recitation + song (like the Lord's Prayer)
   - Each "We believe" should carry equal weight
   - The Christological center (Jesus section) is the longest—don't rush it

2. GUITAR STYLE:
   - Steady, firm fingerpicking
   - F major is grounded and strong
   - Consistent rhythm is essential—this is doctrinal, not free-floating
   - Nylon strings work well

3. RECORDING APPROACH:
   - This is LONG (7-8 minutes)
   - Record 2-3 guitar takes (consistency matters over this length)
   - Vocal: 2-3 full takes (this is substantial)
   - Comp for clarity and steadiness
   - A second voice (counterpart) could harmonize parts (optional)

4. MICROPHONE SETUP:
   - Vocal: 8 inches (clear, forward position)
   - Guitar: 2-3 feet (steady accompaniment)
   - Record in a space with moderate acoustics (not dead, not echo-heavy)

5. MIXING:
   - Vocal: forward (3-4 dB above guitar)
   - Vocal reverb: Room, 12-15% (clarity is important—can't mumble doctrine)
   - Guitar: dry or minimal (8%)
   - Master: clear, strong, affirmative quality
   - NO compression (preserve the declaration's strength)

6. EXPORT:
   - .wav preferred (length and importance warrant lossless)
   - AAC 256 kbps minimum
   - File name: "Nicene_Creed_Steve_Price_[DATE]"

HISTORICAL SIGNIFICANCE:

The Nicene Creed (325 AD) is the oldest continuous Christian statement of faith.
It was written to counter theological errors (Arianism—denying Christ's divinity).

Key theological points:
- "One God" (monotheism)
- "God from God, Light from Light, true God from true God"
  (Christ's full divinity)
- "Begotten, not made, of one Being with the Father"
  (against Arianism—Christ is eternal, not created)
- "His kingdom will have no end" (eternal reign)

This creed is confessed in Catholic, Orthodox, Anglican, and most Protestant
churches every Sunday. It's the most universally accepted Christian statement.
