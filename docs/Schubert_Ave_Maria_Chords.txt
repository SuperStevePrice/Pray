FULL LYRICS (English)

Hail Mary, full of grace,
The Lord is with thee.
Blessed art thou among women,
And blessed is the fruit of thy womb, Jesus.
Holy Mary, Mother of God,
Pray for us sinners,
Now and at the hour of our death. Amen.

HARMONIC ANALYSIS:

The piece is extremely simple harmonically—mostly Bb major, Gm (vi), Cm (ii), 
and F (V). The modal shift to Cm gives it a contemplative, gentle quality.

Key harmonic moments:
- Bb → Gm: Opens the soundscape
- Gm → Cm: Introduces minor color (introspective)
- Cm → F: Movement toward resolution
- F → Bb: Return to home

GARAGEBAND RECORDING TIPS:

1. SETUP:
   - Create a new song, 3/4 time signature, ~65 BPM
   - Add two tracks: Acoustic Guitar + Vocal

2. GUITAR TRACK:
   - Use "Warm Finger Picking" or "Soft Nylon" guitar sound
   - Record multiple takes; keep the best or comp from 2-3
   - Light touch—this is a prayer, not a performance piece
   - Use capo on 1st fret if you prefer open chord voicings (Cm becomes Bm, etc.)

3. VOCAL TRACK:
   - Record in a quiet space, early morning is ideal
   - Don't oversing—Schubert needs restraint and beauty
   - Breathe naturally between phrases
   - Multiple takes: one for verse, one for repeat; you can comp the best parts
   - Add a touch of reverb (Room or Cathedral, ~20% wet) for the spacious effect

4. STRUCTURE FOR GARAGEBAND:
   - Intro: 2 measures of guitar fingerpicking (establish the feel)
   - Verse 1: Guitar + Vocal (12 measures)
   - Verse 2: Vocal alone or lighter guitar (12 measures)
   - Verse 3: Full (guitar + vocal) + gentle crescendo
   - Outro: 2 measures of guitar fading

5. EFFECTS STRATEGY:
   - Guitar: No effects needed, or light reverb (10%)
   - Vocal: Subtle reverb (Cathedral, 15-20%), no compression if possible
   - Master: Keep it clean and intimate—no loudness limiting
   
   Mixing target: Vocal and guitar at equal perceived loudness

6. EXPORT:
   - AAC 256 kbps or .wav (lossless) if you want to preserve quality
   - Name: "Schubert_Ave_Maria_Steve_Price_[DATE]"
   - Check mono/stereo: stereo is richer for guitar + voice

PERFORMANCE NOTES:

- This is a *lied* (German art song)—intimate, not grand
- Tempo: Don't rush. Each verse should feel like one long breath
- Phrasing: "Ave Maria" is the question; "gratia plena" is the response
- Dynamics: Gentle crescendo into verse 2, soften for verse 3
- The simplicity is the point—no shredding, no drama
